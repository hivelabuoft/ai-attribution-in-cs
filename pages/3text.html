In a third-year algorithms course, the assignment was to produce detailed pseudocode for Dijkstra's algorithm. The student began from scratch, working from their understanding from lectures and the textbook. Using a simple text editor without any AI assistance, they carefully authored the algorithm line-by-line. The core of their work involved the direct production of the design artifact, defining the data structures for the priority queue and graph representation, and specifying the main loop's logic. This manual process of writing the pseudocode constituted the entire deliverable, translating the conceptual algorithm into a formal, pre-implementation plan.
