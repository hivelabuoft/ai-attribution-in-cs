In a UX design course, a student began with their own complete and logical user flow diagram for a food delivery app but felt the checkout process was too complex. To get ideas for refinement, they used an AI assistant for an explanation of Jakob Nielsen's usability heuristics, specifically those on minimizing user memory load. After understanding the general principle, the student began the production task of manually redesigning the payment section. This required significant effort to integrate the concept, as they had to reconcile the simplified flow with features like 'apply coupon' and 'guest checkout'. They redrew and evaluated several versions before finalizing a new design that consolidated three screens into one.
